## 1. The Partnership Landscape — Four Pathways

`Source: Official Notion partner and affiliate pages | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026 | Confidence: High`

Notion offers four distinct ways to build a formal relationship with the platform. They are not mutually exclusive — multiple tracks can and often should be pursued simultaneously.

| Pathway | What it is | Revenue model | Who it's for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Solutions Partner — Consulting** | Certified implementation consultant; builds, optimizes, and scales Notion for clients | Service fees from clients | Consultants, agencies, freelancers delivering Notion implementations |
| **Solutions Partner — Sales** | Authorized Notion license reseller; manages customer billing and renewals | Revenue share on license sales | Companies with dedicated sales capacity and iARR commitments |
| **Ambassador** | Community advocate; teaches, creates content, hosts events | Perks (not direct revenue); enables monetization of services and templates | Creators, educators, community builders |
| **Affiliate** | Referral link holder; earns per activated signup + revenue share | Up to $50/activated signup + 20% of year-one revenue per referral | Any active Notion user with an audience |

**The strategic read for this workspace:** The Consulting track is the primary formal partnership target. The Ambassador program runs in parallel as the community and content layer. The Affiliate program is the immediate low-friction monetization vehicle while the partnership builds. The Sales track is not the right fit for a solo creator/operator context — it requires dedicated FTEs and iARR commitments designed for agencies and resellers.

## 2. The Certification Ladder — Required Foundation

`Source: Official Notion Academy + Badges & Certifications help page | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026 | Confidence: High`

Certifications are the foundation every partnership track requires. The ladder has four levels — three free, one paid, one partner-gated.

### Level 1–3: Free Badges (Open to all)

| Badge | Focus | Format | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Essentials** | Foundational Notion skills — pages, blocks, navigation | 20-question quiz, 80% pass required | Free |
| **Workflows** | Collaborative workspace skills — databases, views, team features | 20-question quiz, 80% pass required | Free |
| **Advanced** | Expert-level capabilities — formulas, automations, complex databases | 20-question quiz, 80% pass required | Free |

All three must be completed before advancing. Pass rate is 80% per quiz. For anyone already operating at Evan's level — enterprise Super Admin, complex formula builds, sprint infra for 40+ person teams — these are straightforward. The questions are drawn from the course material and are multiple-choice. Treat them as fast credential gathering, not learning events.

### Level 4: Administrator Certification (Open to all, paid)

| Certification | Focus | Format | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Administrator** | Core platform management and governance — security, permissions, workspace admin | Proctored exam (webcam + screen share), 80% pass required | $100–$150 |

This is the gateway credential. Required before any partner certification. Covers workspace governance, enterprise permissions, security settings, user management — areas where the existing enterprise Super Admin experience is directly applicable. The proctored exam format (monitored via webcam and screen sharing) reflects that this is a real credential, not a quiz.

**Recommendation:** Complete all three free badges in a single sitting, then schedule the Administrator Certification. This clears the entire foundational path in one sprint.

### Level 5: Service Specialist Certification (Partner-gated)

| Certification | Focus | Format | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Service Specialist** | Consulting, project management, client engagements, enterprise implementation | Proctored exam + practical exercises | Partner-gated |

Only available after Solutions Partner application is approved. Covers topics oriented toward larger clients with complex implementations — the practical exercises add meaningful depth beyond the multiple-choice format. This certification officially confirms Consulting Partner status.

### Upcoming certifications (announced, not yet released as of March 2026)

`Source: Official Notion help — "coming soon" | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026`

- **Sales Specialist Certification:** Aimed at license resellers — solution positioning and business value articulation. Scheduled Q1 2026 but not yet released.
- **Technical Specialist Certification:** Advanced customization and integration, requires Administrator first. No confirmed release date.

Watch for these — Technical Specialist in particular will be relevant as the workspace and agent infrastructure become demonstrable expertise.

## 3. Solutions Partner — Consulting Track

`Source: Official Notion Solutions Partner page + Help Center | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026 | Confidence: High`

### What it is

Consulting Partners help organizations adopt and scale Notion by delivering services: consulting, implementation, integrations, training, and ongoing managed support. They are listed in the official Notion partner directory, which is how enterprise clients find them.

### Requirements to apply

- Create a partner profile and accept the Solutions Partner Agreement
- Demonstrate prior work building Notion systems (documented case studies, client examples)
- Pass application review by Notion's team

**Common rejection reasons (per Notion guidance):**
- Previous work examples are not convincing
- Services are not well-defined
- Insufficient experience or bandwidth to deliver at enterprise scale
- Too many existing applicants in the region/industry

### Requirements after approval

- Complete the three free badges (Essentials, Workflows, Advanced)
- Pass the Administrator Certification ($100–$150, proctored)
- Pass the Service Specialist Certification (partner-gated, available post-approval)
- Meet client experience requirements (defined by Notion; involves demonstrating real client delivery)
- Complete partner profile to be listed in the directory

### Revenue model

Consulting Partners set their own rates for services. Notion does not take a cut of service revenue — the partnership provides credibility, directory listing, early feature access, and direct Notion support. Revenue comes entirely from client engagements.

### What the directory listing unlocks

Being listed in the Notion partner directory means enterprise clients searching for implementation help find you. Notion's enterprise sales team also refers clients to directory partners when they need implementation support. This is the distribution mechanism — the certification earns the credential, the directory listing generates the inbound.

## 4. Solutions Partner — Sales Track

`Source: Official Notion help | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026 | Confidence: High`

### What it is

Sales Partners manage the full customer lifecycle for Notion licenses — sourcing new customers, facilitating expansions, managing renewals, and providing account oversight. They earn revenue share on license sales.

### Requirements

- Apply to become a Sales Partner
- Demonstrate a proven track record building and scaling practices for other SaaS platforms
- **Commit to iARR (Incremental Annual Recurring Revenue) targets** with quarterly plans and reviews
- **Assign named, full-time FTEs dedicated to selling Notion**
- Earn required badges and certifications (certifications must be held by unique individuals — no double-counting)

### Why this track is not the right fit here

The Sales track is designed for consulting firms and agencies with dedicated Notion sales capacity. The iARR commitment and required full-time FTE assignments are structural requirements designed for multi-person operations. For a solo operator or small creator business, these requirements create obligations without the infrastructure to fulfill them. The Consulting track provides partnership recognition, directory listing, and enterprise access without these constraints.

## 5. Ambassador Program

`Source: Official Notion community + verified secondary sources | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026 | Confidence: Medium — program details subject to change; verify at notion.com/community before applying`

### What it is

Ambassadors are community advocates formally recognized by Notion. They teach Notion, create content, host events (online and offline), build templates, provide feedback, and represent the platform in their communities. The program is volunteer-based — Ambassadors are not paid by Notion for their time.

### Requirements

- Active Notion user with demonstrated community involvement
- Ability to teach Notion to others
- Commit 2–3 hours per quarter in service of the community
- Participate in a private Slack group
- Applications open periodically (not continuously) — engage with the community before applying; Notion selects from users already visibly contributing

### What Ambassadors receive

- Early access to new features
- Exclusive training and briefings
- Networking with other Ambassadors
- Branded swag
- Direct support from Notion's team
- Formal recognition on Notion's community surfaces

### What Ambassadors do NOT receive

- Direct payment from Notion for community work
- Exclusive revenue share (separate from affiliate program)
- Guaranteed listing in business directory (that's the Solutions Partner program)

### Strategic role in the partnership stack

The Ambassador program is the **community and visibility layer** — it creates public presence, early feature access, and direct Notion team relationships. It complements the Consulting track, which is the **revenue and enterprise layer**. Running both simultaneously is explicitly supported and strategically optimal: the Ambassador relationship feeds the Consulting pipeline through visibility and credibility.

### How to position for Ambassador selection

Ambassadors are selected from people already visibly contributing — not from cold applications. The path to selection is:

1. Build in public: publish Notion tutorials, templates, content about the workspace being built
2. Engage with the community: Notion subreddits, X/Twitter, official community forums
3. Create templates and share them (on the Marketplace — see Section 8)
4. Document expertise publicly: the Codex itself, stream content about Notion, educational posts
5. Apply when a cohort opens — Notion reviews cohorts periodically, not rolling

## 6. Affiliate Program

`Source: Official Notion affiliate page | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026 | Confidence: High`

### What it is

The Notion Affiliate Program is the simplest and most immediately accessible revenue pathway. Anyone can apply.

### Terms

- **Per activated signup:** Up to $50
- **Year-one revenue share:** 20% of year-one revenue for each referral
- **No cap:** No limit on total earnings

### Why activate this now

The affiliate program is the right move regardless of which partnership track is being pursued. It monetizes existing Notion advocacy that's already happening — content about Notion, the Codex itself if published, stream sessions about workspace building, template marketplace listings. The activation cost is near zero and the link can be added to any existing content surface.

**Where to place the affiliate link:**
- Notion template marketplace listings (each template description)
- Content about Notion workflow on YouTube, Substack, social
- Any public-facing Notion workspace or guide
- Stream overlays or stream description when covering Notion content

## 8. Template Marketplace Strategy

`Source: Official Notion Marketplace + verified creator community | Last verified: Mar 4, 2026`

### Why templates matter for partnership

Templates are the most visible public evidence of Notion expertise. Every template listing is a credential — it demonstrates the ability to build useful, polished systems. For the Solutions Partner application, template examples are among the strongest portfolio items. For Ambassador selection, template publishing is explicitly listed as a qualifying activity.

Templates also generate revenue: Notion's native marketplace accepts payments via native Notion payments (USD only, 10% + $0.40 per transaction), or through linked third-party platforms (Gumroad: 10% + $0.50; Easytools: ~5–7% total including Stripe).

### What to publish from this workspace

The Codex suite itself is not the template — it's proprietary strategy. But the *systems* it describes are publishable as standalone templates. High-signal template categories based on what's been built:

| Template idea | Target buyer | Complexity | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Creator OS** — Content calendar + CRM + Projects (streamlined) | Solo creators, streamers | Medium | High |
| **Sprint infrastructure template** — Full sprint system (Doc 1 architecture) | Small teams, agile practitioners | High | High |
| **AI-native workspace starter** — Agent infrastructure stack + Autofill setup | Power users ready for agents | High | High |
| **Commission tracker for creators** — Commissions + Asset Library (Doc 7) | Artists, creators with collaborators | Medium | Medium |
| **Content calendar with Autofill** — Editorial pipeline (Doc 4) | Content creators, marketers | Medium | Medium |
| **Freelancer CRM** — Contacts + pipeline (Doc 2 simplified) | Independent consultants, freelancers | Low | Medium |

### Template quality standards (what makes a template sell)

- **Solves a specific, named problem** — not a generic dashboard
- **Includes a setup guide** in the template itself (page body instructions for new users)
- **Prefilled with example data** — buyers should immediately see what the system looks like in use
- **Includes views that demonstrate the workflow** — don't just show a table; show the pipeline in Board view, the calendar, the gallery
- **Property schemas are clean and typed correctly** — Status properties not Select, Relations not Text
- **Mobile-compatible** — test every view on mobile before publishing
- **Cover image and thumbnail** — visual first impression drives clicks on the marketplace

### Pricing guidance

- Simple templates (single database, minimal setup): $7–$15
- Medium templates (multi-database, 3–5 views, setup guide): $15–$35
- Complex systems (full OS, multiple databases, agent setup guides): $35–$97
- Bundles (multiple related templates): $50–$150

Price based on problem value, not time to build. A sprint infrastructure template that saves a team lead 10 hours of setup is worth $35+ regardless of how fast it was built.

### Listing strategy

1. **Notion Marketplace first** — the native marketplace is where Notion users are. Visibility without marketing.
2. **Gumroad or Easytools for owned audience** — embed in content, newsletters, stream descriptions. Better margins than native marketplace.
3. **Cross-promote via content** — every template published is content: "How I built this," demos on stream, walkthroughs as YouTube videos

## 9. Notion for Creators — Public Positioning

The Notion for Creators positioning is the public-facing brand layer that feeds all partnership tracks. It's not a formal Notion program — it's the content and community identity that connects the workspace building work to a visible public audience.

### The core positioning

**What you are:** A creator-operator who builds enterprise-grade Notion systems for solo use, publishes the playbook, and teaches what actually works at the intersection of AI agents, content workflows, and creative production — not just productivity theory.

**What differentiates this from generic Notion content:**
- Enterprise Super Admin context — most Notion creators haven't scaled to 150-person orgs
- AI-native workspace design from day one — not retrofitting AI onto old systems
- Active production use — the systems documented here are in live use, not theoretical
- Creator-operator overlap — building a streaming/writing/art brand while running the ops; the Codex is real infrastructure, not a thought experiment
- Technical depth — HTML overlays, agent SOPs, formula 2.0, MCP layer

### Content strategy for partnership evidence

Every piece of public Notion content simultaneously serves three purposes: audience building, Ambassador candidacy, and Solutions Partner portfolio evidence.

**High-value content formats for this positioning:**

| Format | Platform | Partnership value |
|---|---|---|
| "How I built [X system] in Notion" walkthroughs | YouTube, stream VODs | Portfolio evidence; Ambassador activity |
| Template demos and teardowns | YouTube, TikTok/Reels | Marketplace traffic; Ambassador activity |
| AI agent setup guides (Notion 3.3 Custom Agents) | YouTube, Substack | Highest current demand; differentiator |
| Codex-derived educational posts | X/Twitter, LinkedIn | Visible community contribution |
| Live workspace builds on stream | Twitch, YouTube | Authentic proof of expertise |

### What NOT to publish publicly from this workspace

The Codex is proprietary strategy — it should not be published in full. It contains competitive thinking, collaborator terms, financial data, and brand methodology that is private by design.

What IS publishable: derived systems, tutorial content, template versions of the databases, "how I think about X" posts that reference the principles without exposing the specific implementation.

**Rule:** Share the methodology. Protect the master.

## 11. The Partnership Tracker — Notion Infrastructure

Track the full partnership journey inside the workspace using this lightweight system.

### Partnership Progress database (minimal schema)

| Property | Type | Options |
|---|---|---|
| **Item** | Title | Certification name, template title, application stage, etc. |
| **Category** | Select | Certification / Template / Application / Community / Content |
| **Status** | Status | To-do / In progress / Submitted / Complete |
| **Target Date** | Date | When this should be done |
| **Notes** | Text | Key details, links, outcomes |
| **Link** | URL | Exam link, submission URL, template listing URL |

### Views

- **Active** (Board): Group by Category, filter Status ≠ Complete — everything in motion
- **Certifications** (Table): Filter Category = Certification — the ladder at a glance
- **Templates** (Table): Filter Category = Template — marketplace portfolio tracker
- **Timeline** (Timeline): Spread across target dates — sequencing view

### Automations (free, native)

- Status changes to Complete → Send Slack notification to #partnership-wins: "✅ {{Item}} complete"
- Status changes to Submitted → Set reminder 14 days out for follow-up

## 13. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | What breaks | The fix |
|---|---|---|
| Applying to Solutions Partner before any templates are published | Application reviewers can't verify public evidence of expertise | Publish at least 2–3 templates before applying; templates are the most verifiable proof |
| Applying to Sales Partner track for a solo operator | iARR commitments and FTE requirements can't be met; rejection and wasted time | Consulting track is the right fit; Sales track requires dedicated agency infrastructure |
| Pursuing Ambassador first before Consulting | Ambassador has no revenue path; Consulting has the directory listing and enterprise access | Apply to Consulting first; run Ambassador in parallel or after |
| Treating free badges as optional | Administrator Certification requires passing all three free badges first; skipping creates a dependency gap | Complete all three free badges in one sitting — they're fast |
| Publishing the Codex publicly as a template | Exposes proprietary strategy, collaborator terms, financial data | Derive template versions of the systems; share methodology, protect implementation |
| Waiting for "perfect" templates before publishing | Delay accumulates; no marketplace evidence for application | Ship a functional template, gather feedback, iterate — done beats perfect |
| Conflating Affiliate, Ambassador, and Solutions Partner | Different programs, different value, different requirements — treating them as the same creates strategic confusion | Each track has a specific role in the stack (see Section 1 table) |
| Not documenting enterprise admin experience for the application | Unique differentiator goes unsaid; application looks like any other creator | Make the enterprise scale explicit — 150+ person org, 40+ person sprint infra, Super Admin — this is rare in the creator Notion space |
| Skipping the Administrator Certification because it costs money | It's the gate to the Service Specialist and partner directory listing; the entire Consulting track depends on it | $100–$150 is the entry fee to a partner track with multi-thousand dollar service revenue potential |
